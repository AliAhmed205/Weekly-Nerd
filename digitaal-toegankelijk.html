Digitaal Toegankelijk

About

During this Weekly Nerd session, Marieke, a researcher in digital accessibility, and Pim, co-founder of Digitaal Toegankelijk, were invited to speak. This session offered an in-depth exploration of digital accessibility, highlighting essential concepts and practical tips to make digital products accessible for everyone.

For everyone

Marieke, a former student who interned at Active Collective, and Pim began their session with some icebreaker questions that emphasized the core of digital accessibility. They explored who likes to be excluded and who one prefers to exclude, paying attention to both temporary and permanent disabilities. This revealed that the design of digital products can make a significant difference for people with disabilities.

WCAG, The Big Four, Low Hanging Fruit, and P.O.U.R

During the presentation, emphasis was placed on the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and its four core principles:

  • Perceivable
  • Operable
  • Understandable
  • Robust

These principles guide the creation of accessible digital content. Perceivable focuses on ensuring all information is perceivable, Operable on guaranteeing usability, Understandable on making content comprehensible, and Robust on creating robust and compatible content. Specific criteria, such as the need for audio control and avoiding flashing elements, were highlighted as crucial for digital accessibility. This explanation provided a deep understanding of the complexity of digital accessibility and the need to consider diverse user needs and capabilities when designing and developing digital content.

Practical Tips

The speakers emphasized the importance of listening to digital products with a screen reader and navigating using only a keyboard to understand and improve the user experience for people with various disabilities. They also encouraged testing with the target audience to uncover and resolve any accessibility issues.
