Fenna de Wilde

About

During the second "Weekly Nerd" session, we welcomed Fenna de Wilde, an exceptionally talented front-end developer who describes herself as "creative." She emphasizes this because she is often involved in the design process and can share her expertise on how the final design can come together.

Accessibility

Fenna graduated from the Hogeschool van Amsterdam and completed her internship at Build in Amsterdam. During her presentation, she shared some valuable insights, which are often covered in our curriculum but can be overlooked in practice: accessibility within websites.

During Fenna's presentation, she emphasized the importance of making websites and apps accessible to everyone, not just those without disabilities. She provided useful tips, such as using focus states, where there are two variants to apply the focus state: :focus & :focus-visible. The main difference here is that ":focus-visible" targets specific elements selected with the keyboard rather than the mouse or touch.

What is Polypane?

Another important aspect Fenna addressed was the use and addition of Aria attributes to enhance accessibility for users who use screen readers. There are numerous possibilities with these attributes to clearly communicate to a screen reader what happens with certain elements. I personally found aria-controls interesting. Aria-controls is used to link a control element to the element it controls. This allows screen readers to understand the connection and provide more context to users. For example, a button, link, or tab. And refer to the ID of the element it manages. For example, dropdown. A brief summary of the treated Aria attributes follows below:

  • Focus state for keyboard users
  • Aria attributes
  • Accessible carousels
  • Focus protection
  • Semantics
  • Color contrast
  • Text size
  • Line height
  • Alternative text description

The Rule of Least Power

In his argument, Valkhof emphasizes the importance of applying the basic principle of "the rule of least power" when building web technologies. This principle advises choosing the least powerful language suitable for a particular purpose. He argues that JavaScript, although powerful, is also vulnerable and delicate. By writing clear HTML, many functionalities can be achieved without the need for JavaScript.

Browser Makers Listen and Implement

One of the key points that Valkhof emphasizes is that browser makers are listening to the needs of developers and implementing functionalities that previously had to be built manually. Modern browsers can now perform many tasks that were previously attributed to JavaScript, but faster and more accessibly.
